Research

I hunt exoplanets using the radial velocity (RV) method, understanding the hide-and-seek between stellar activity and planetary signals.
Based in Birmingham, I am expanding the Multi-Mask Least Squares Deconvolution (MM LSD) pipeline, extracting extremely precise radial velocity (EPRV) measurements to elevate planet detection capabilities.

I lead a dedicated international team of scientists to investigate the spectral signature of super/granulation —stellar surface phenomena driven by convection flows on stars that can affect planet detection— on stars across spectral types.

I developed a pipeline called 'TWEAKS', to disentangle the stellar variability from RV measurements, and push the detection limits to low-mass exoplanets.
As a member of the HARPS-N consortium and the PLATO Stellar Variability Working Group, I contribute to pioneering methodologies that shape exoplanet characterization and stellar activity modelling.

Outreach

I believe science is boundless, and it should be accessible to everyone, regardless of age, race, color, language, gender, or country of origin.
As a scientist, I see it as a fundamental duty to make this a reality. I actively engage with the public, especially the younger generation, to share the frontiers of my research and inspire curiosity.
It brings me joy to chat with primary and secondary school students through 'I'm a Scientist' sessions. I am also Royal Astronomical Society's STEM Ambassador. Previously, I have worked as a mentor for the 'ASTROPHYSICAST' team, a group of young astronomy enthusiasts, focusing on delivering accessible Astronomy outreach events at schools in India.
